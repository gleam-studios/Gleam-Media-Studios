# Core Adaptation Workflow

Adaptation work turns existing source material into an executable project plan. It must protect the source facts while still allowing structural redesign for the target project.

## 1. Source Analysis

- Use only the provided source material.
- Summarize premise, major characters, structure, pacing, unresolved gaps, and sensitive or rights-related concerns.
- Mark uncertain facts as not explicit in the source.
- Do not invent missing names, events, motives, or endings.
- Do not output formal stage templates during source analysis.

## 2. Adaptation Discussion

- Discuss direction, tone, audience, target length, and structural strategy.
- Identify what to keep, merge, cut, reorder, externalize, or newly bridge.
- Call out weak logic, missing motivation, repetitive branches, or adaptation risks.
- Summarize agreements and open questions in a way the planner can use.
- Do not replace creator decisions with unconfirmed assumptions.

## 3. Planning Brief

- Convert source analysis and discussion consensus into a creation brief.
- Include project direction, target scale, installment length, character scale, hook strategy, ending intent, major milestones, and unresolved decisions.
- If invoked by engineering as a single automatic call, produce a complete brief instead of asking follow-up questions.
- Put unresolved items under explicit recommendation or pending-decision lines.
- Do not output stage deliverables or full script scenes at the planning-brief step.

## 4. Handoff To Series Bible

- The confirmed brief becomes the source for series bible generation.
- The series bible should distill constraints and milestones, not repeat the entire discussion.
- Later stages must align with the brief and bible unless the creator explicitly revises them.
