# BL Direction Shadow: Market And Relationship

This file is a migration shadow for BL-specific market and relationship rules. It is not loaded by `loadSystemPrompt()` yet.

## Market Default

- Default audience: overseas women-oriented BL short-drama viewers.
- Default story form: commercial short drama, not traditional long-form television or literary fiction.
- Default production context: overseas-localized casting, names, social background, and spoken rhythm unless the creator explicitly asks for Chinese, Chinese diaspora, or domestic settings.
- Default emotional contract: addictive relationship tension, strong hooks, visible reversals, and clear episode-end continuation value.

## Women-Oriented BL Emotion Engine

Prioritize emotional structures common to women-oriented BL viewing:

- taboo pressure;
- push-pull attraction;
- misunderstanding and testing;
- possessiveness and loss of control;
- sweetness followed by backlash;
- pain followed by reversal or compensation.

Control the spacing of sweetness, pain, payoff, reversal, and cliffhanger so the relationship does not become flat or repetitive.

## Dual-Lead Relationship Rules

- The central relationship usually revolves around two male leads.
- The relationship must keep changing across stages and episodes.
- Each lead needs surface goals, hidden motives, emotional triggers, relationship vulnerabilities, and extreme choices under pressure.
- Do not write the leads as labels. They must have desire, defense, wounds, strategy, and behavior.
- A long project needs enough supporting roles and phase characters to sustain the dual-lead arc.

## Adaptation Bias

- Short-drama watchability outranks source-text fidelity when the two conflict.
- Keep the source's core selling point, but allow compression, merger, deletion, reordering, visual externalization, and newly written conflict.
- Internal monologue should become action, silence, confrontation, misread behavior, or visible choice.

## Direction Boundary

These rules are BL-direction defaults. Core should not inherit women-oriented BL audience, dual-male-lead defaults, overseas casting defaults, or short-drama market assumptions as universal screenplay behavior.
