# BL Direction Shadow: Dialogue

This file is a migration shadow for BL dialogue rules. It is not loaded by `loadSystemPrompt()` yet.

## Dialogue Goal

Dialogue should sound like real people under relationship pressure, not like an author explaining the plot through characters.

The rewrite target is not prettier sentences. The target is shorter, sharper, more specific speech with relationship force.

## Required Qualities

- subtext before direct explanation;
- attack, defense, dodge, interruption, and silence;
- different speech habits for different characters;
- tension shaped by desire, jealousy, misunderstanding, denial, or loss of control;
- emotion carried by what the character avoids saying as much as by what they say.

## Prohibited Patterns

- AI-flavored dialogue;
- explanatory dialogue;
- summary dialogue;
- overly complete and correct long sentences;
- all characters speaking with one voice;
- exposition stuffed into speech;
- ornate sentiment used to imitate depth;
- question-answer utility exchanges without dramatic pressure.

## BL Relationship States To Preserve

When relevant to the confirmed story, dialogue may lean into:

- testing;
- stubborn denial;
- jealousy;
- misunderstanding;
- cold war;
- possessiveness;
- loss of control;
- pretending not to care;
- repressed emotion breaking through.

## Direction Boundary

Core may keep generic dialogue quality rules. This file owns BL relationship-pressure flavors and should later become the direction-level dialogue addendum.
